Tomato Rasam

Description

A comforting and aromatic South Indian soup, bursting with the flavors of tomatoes, tamarind, and a blend of spices. Perfect as a light soup or served with rice.

Ingredients

  • 2 large ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 small gooseberry-sized ball of tamarind (or 1 tbsp tamarind paste)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

For Tempering:

  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 dried red chilies, broken
  • A few curry leaves
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Soak tamarind in 1/4 cup warm water for 10 minutes. Squeeze out pulp and discard solids. If using paste, dilute with 1/4 cup water.
  2. Step 2: In a pot, combine chopped tomatoes, tamarind extract (or diluted paste), turmeric powder, salt, and 3.5 cups of water.
  3. Step 3: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, until tomatoes are soft and well cooked. Gently mash the tomatoes with the back of a spoon.
  4. Step 4: Meanwhile, prepare the tempering. In a small pan, heat ghee or oil. Add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add cumin seeds, dried red chilies, curry leaves, and crushed garlic (if using). Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
  5. Step 5: Pour the tempering into the simmering rasam. Add asafoetida and black pepper powder (if using).
  6. Step 6: Simmer for another 2-3 minutes. Do not over-boil after adding tempering.
  7. Step 7: Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto baking sheets.
  8. Step 8: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Servings: 4
