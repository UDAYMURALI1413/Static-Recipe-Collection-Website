Vegetable Sambar

Description

A staple South Indian lentil and vegetable stew, Sambar is wholesome, flavorful, and commonly served with idli, dosa, rice, or vada.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup Toor dal (split pigeon peas), washed and soaked for 20 minutes
  • 3 cups mixed vegetables, chopped (e.g., drumsticks, carrots, potato, pumpkin, beans, eggplant)
  • 1.5 tablespoons Sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 3-4 cups water

For Tempering:

  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • 2-3 dried red chilies, broken
  • A few curry leaves
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Pressure cook soaked toor dal with 2 cups of water and turmeric powder for 3-4 whistles until soft and mushy. Mash well.
  2. Step 2: Soak tamarind in 1/2 cup warm water for 10 minutes. Squeeze out pulp and discard solids. If using paste, dilute with 1/2 cup water.
  3. Step 3: In a separate pot, cook the chopped mixed vegetables with 1 cup of water and a pinch of salt until tender (about 10-15 minutes).
  4. Step 4: Add the mashed toor dal to the cooked vegetables. Add tamarind extract (or diluted paste) and sambar powder. Add more water if needed to reach desired consistency (usually a thick soup).
  5. Step 5: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing flavors to meld. Adjust salt.
  6. Step 6: For tempering, heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds. Once mustard splutters, add asafoetida, dried red chilies, curry leaves, and sliced onion (if using). Saut√© until onion is translucent.
  7. Step 7: Pour the tempering into the simmering sambar. Mix gently.
  8. Step 8: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves (optional) and serve hot.

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 30-40 minutes

Servings: 4-5
