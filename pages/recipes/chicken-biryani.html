Chicken Biryani



Description

A classic, aromatic chicken and rice dish layered with spices, herbs, and caramelized onions. This flavorful biryani is a showstopper main course perfect for festive occasions or weekend family meals.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) chicken, cut into pieces (bone-in preferred)
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 3 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala or garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 5-6 cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 2-3 cinnamon sticks
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds (optional)
  • 1/2 cup milk, warmed with a pinch of saffron (optional)
  • 1/4 cup ghee or vegetable oil (divided, plus more as needed)
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon juice for taste

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: ombine chicken, yogurt, 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste, half the mint and coriander, half the biryani masala, chili powder, turmeric, salt, and a squeeze of lemon in a large bowl. Mix well. Cover and marinate for at least 1 hour (or overnight in the fridge)
  2. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice several times and soak in water for 30 minutes.
  3. Fry Onions & Sear Chicken: In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons oil/ghee. Fry the sliced onions until golden brown and caramelized. Set aside half for layering.

    In the remaining oil, add whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves, cumin seeds if using). Sauté for 1 minute.

    Add marinated chicken and cook on medium-high heat for 5-7 minutes until sealed. Add the remaining ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, and green chilies. Cook until tomatoes soften and chicken is about 60-70% cooked

  4. Cook Rice: Meanwhile, boil 6-8 cups of water with salt in a large pot. Add soaked, drained rice. Boil until rice is 70-80% cooked (it should have a bite). Drain immediately.
  5. Layer the Biryani: In the pot with cooked chicken, spread half the cooked rice evenly. Sprinkle some reserved fried onions, mint, coriander, biryani masala, and drizzle some saffron milk if using.

    Repeat with the remaining rice and toppings. Optionally, finish with melted ghee and a sprinkle of black pepper.

  6. Dum Cooking (Steaming): Seal the pot tightly with foil or cover with a heavy lid. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes for the flavors to meld and rice to steam (“dum” method).

    Remove from heat and rest for another 10 minutes before opening.

  7. Serve: Fluff up the biryani gently. Serve hot, garnished with extra fried onions and fresh herbs. Enjoy with raita or lemon wedges.

Prep Time: 20 minutes (plus marination time)

Cook Time: 45-60 minutes

Servings: 5-6

Tips :

For best results, use long-grain basmati rice and bone-in chicken, and marinate the meat overnight for deep flavor.
