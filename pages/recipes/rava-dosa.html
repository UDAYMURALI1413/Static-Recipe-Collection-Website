Crispy Rava Dosa

Description

A quick and crispy South Indian crepe made from semolina, rice flour, and plain flour, spiced with green chilies and ginger. Does not require fermentation.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup fine semolina (rava/sooji)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns, crushed
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion (optional)
  • A few curry leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 2.5 - 3 cups water (adjust as needed for a very thin batter)
  • Oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. Step 1: In a large bowl, combine semolina, rice flour, and all-purpose flour.
  2. Step 2: Add cumin seeds, crushed black peppercorns, chopped green chili, grated ginger, chopped onion (if using), curry leaves, and salt.
  3. Step 3: Gradually add water, whisking continuously to avoid lumps, until you have a very thin, watery batter. It should be much thinner than regular dosa batter. Let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Step 4: Heat a non-stick tawa or cast-iron griddle over medium-high heat. Grease lightly with oil.
  5. Step 5: Before pouring each dosa, stir the batter well as the flours tend to settle at the bottom.
  6. Step 6: Pour a ladleful of batter from the outer edge towards the center, covering the tawa in a lacy pattern. Fill in any gaps. You should hear a sizzling sound.
  7. Step 7: Drizzle a teaspoon of oil around the edges and over the dosa.
  8. Step 8: Cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy (about 2-3 minutes). You may need to adjust heat to prevent burning. The top will look cooked and slightly dry.
  9. Step 9: Gently loosen the edges with a spatula and fold the dosa in half.
  10. Step 10: Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 3-4 minutes per Dosa

Servings: 4-5 Dosas
