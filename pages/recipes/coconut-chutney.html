Simple Coconut Chutney

Description

A classic South Indian condiment, this simple coconut chutney is creamy, mildly spicy, and perfectly complements dosa, idli, vada, or uttapam.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh grated coconut (or frozen, thawed)
  • 1-2 green chilies (adjust to spice preference)
  • 1 inch ginger, roughly chopped
  • 1/4 cup roasted chana dal (dalia, optional, for thickness)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup water (or as needed for blending)

For Tempering:

  • 1 teaspoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1-2 dried red chilies, broken
  • A few curry leaves

Instructions:

  1. Step 1: In a blender, combine grated coconut, green chilies, ginger, roasted chana dal (if using), and salt.
  2. Step 2: Add 1/4 cup water and blend until smooth. Add more water, a tablespoon at a time, if needed to achieve a smooth, but not watery, consistency.
  3. Step 3: Transfer the chutney to a serving bowl.
  4. Step 4: For tempering, heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add asafoetida, dried red chilies, and curry leaves. Saut√© for a few seconds until fragrant.
  5. Step 5: Pour the tempering over the prepared chutney. Mix gently.
  6. Step 6: Serve fresh.

Prep Time: 5 minutes

Cook Time: 5 minutes

Servings: 4
